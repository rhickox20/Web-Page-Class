Valentina Tereshkova: First Woman in Space

Fun Facts

She was an amateur skydiver before her recruitment


In the opening ceremony of the 2014 Olympics, she carried the Olympic flag


Her father, Vladimir Tereshkova, was a war hero during World War II


Per her request, the government searched for the exact area her father died, and placed a monument in his honor


She had two husbands, Andriyan Nikolayev, whom she divorced, and Yuliy G. Shaposhnikov

Biography

Valentina Tereshkova was born in Central Russia, on March 6th, 1937. Her father was a tractor driver and her mother worked in a textile plant. She went to school at age 8 until age 16. Before becoming a cosmonaut, she was a textile-factory worker and an amateur skydiver. She held various political offices in the Communist Party of the Soviet Union. Tereshkova had been very interested in parachuting and trained in skydiving, first jumping at the age of 22. Her skydiving expertise lead her to be selected as a cosmonaut.


Valentina Tereshkova is a retired Russian cosmonaut, engineer, and politician. Tereshkova was selected out of 400 applicants to pilot Vostok 6 in June of 1963. With 3 days in space, she completed 48 orbits of the Earth. She was inducted into the Soviet Air Force, and became the first woman to fly in space, on the Vostok 6. The work, interest, and persistence of Tereshkova opened the opportunity for other women to fly in space.


Tereshkova is known for saying "Hey sky, take off your hat. I’m on my way!” Her work was acknowledged by being given the title: Hero of the Soviet Union. She was further remembered in 2015, for the Vostok 6, that was put on display at the Science Museum in London.

Achievements

With one flight, she logged more flight time than the combined times of all American astronauts who had flown before


Had a minor planet named after her for her outstanding work: Chaika


Order of Merit for the Fatherland: Outstanding contribution to space


Order of Honor: For strengthening scientific and cultural ties


Tereshkova was invited to Prime Minister's residence on her 70th birthday, due to her outstanding work in space.
